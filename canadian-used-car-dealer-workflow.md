# Canadian Used Car Dealer Workflow

This guide documents a practical used-car buying workflow for Canada. The goal is to turn online vehicle data, market data, and inspection results into a disciplined buy/pass decision and a maximum purchase price.

## Core Principle

A dealer should not ask, "Can I buy this car?"

The better question is:

"Can I buy this car at a price where the resale value, repair risk, selling time, and profit margin make sense?"

The profit is usually made before buying the car, not after listing it.

## Step 1: Identify the Exact Vehicle

Before pricing or negotiating, confirm the exact vehicle profile.

### Information to collect

- VIN
- Year
- Make
- Model
- Trim
- Engine
- Transmission
- Drivetrain
- Body style
- Mileage
- Exterior and interior color
- Ownership type: private seller, dealer, lease return, auction, trade-in
- Location and province of registration

### How to access this information

| Information | How to access it |
| --- | --- |
| VIN | Ask the seller for a photo of the VIN plate, registration, ownership, or dashboard VIN. Also verify it physically on the vehicle. |
| Year, make, model, trim | Decode the VIN through CARFAX Canada, dealer inventory tools, OEM VIN decoders, or auction listing details. |
| Mileage | Seller listing, dashboard photo, auction condition report, CARFAX Canada records, and physical inspection. |
| Color and options | Seller photos, window sticker if available, OEM VIN decoder, dealer listing, or physical inspection. |
| Province history | CARFAX Canada registration history and provincial documents where applicable. |

### Dealer notes

Do not price a vehicle using only year/make/model. Trim, drivetrain, mileage, color, accident history, and local demand can change value by thousands of dollars.

## Step 2: Run Vehicle History and Title Checks

Vehicle history determines whether the car is retail-ready, risky, or should be avoided.

### Information to collect

- Accident claims
- Repair claim amounts
- Registration history
- Number of owners
- Title/brand status
- Odometer records
- Service history
- U.S. import history
- Theft or recovery records
- Total loss, salvage, rebuilt, flood, fire, or irreparable status

### How to access this information

| Information | How to access it |
| --- | --- |
| Accident history | CARFAX Canada vehicle history report using the VIN. |
| Claim amounts | CARFAX Canada, insurance records if seller provides them, and sometimes auction condition reports. |
| Registration history | CARFAX Canada and provincial ownership documents. |
| Number of owners | CARFAX Canada and provincial used vehicle documents where available. |
| Title/brand status | CARFAX Canada, provincial motor vehicle registry, auction disclosures, and seller ownership documents. |
| Odometer history | CARFAX Canada mileage events, service records, auction reports, and inspection. |
| Service records | CARFAX Canada, seller invoices, dealership service printouts, and maintenance booklet. |
| U.S. import history | CARFAX Canada, U.S. CARFAX or AutoCheck if available, border/import documents, and registration history. |
| Theft/flood/salvage signals | CARFAX Canada, IBC VIN Verify, NICB VINCheck for U.S.-linked vehicles, and provincial branding records. |

### Dealer notes

A clean CARFAX does not replace inspection. Some damage is repaired privately and never appears on a history report.

Be especially cautious with:

- Recent registration changes
- Missing mileage events
- U.S. imports with incomplete history
- Rebuilt or branded titles
- Large accident claims
- Structural damage disclosures
- Flood or water damage signals

## Step 3: Check Liens and Ownership Risk

A car with a lien can create legal and financial problems after purchase.

### Information to collect

- Active liens
- Financing/security interests
- Seller name matching ownership
- Province-specific ownership requirements
- Whether the seller has legal authority to sell

### How to access this information

| Information | How to access it |
| --- | --- |
| Active lien | CARFAX Canada lien check and the applicable provincial PPSA/PPSR registry. |
| Ontario ownership and lien details | Ontario Used Vehicle Information Package, also called UVIP. |
| Seller identity | Compare seller ID, vehicle ownership/registration, and bill of sale details. |
| Dealer or auction title status | Auction title documents, dealer disclosure, and bill of sale. |
| Loan payout status | Ask seller for lender payout letter and confirm payment process directly with lender when possible. |

### Dealer notes

Never rely only on the seller saying "there is no lien." Run the lien check before paying. If a lien exists, structure payment so the lender is paid out directly and the seller only receives the balance.

## Step 4: Check Recalls and Compliance

Open recalls can affect resale readiness and buyer confidence.

### Information to collect

- Open safety recalls
- Recall completion status
- OEM campaign history
- Canadian compliance for imports

### How to access this information

| Information | How to access it |
| --- | --- |
| Open recalls | Transport Canada recalls database, CARFAX Canada recall check, and manufacturer recall lookup by VIN. |
| Recall completion | Call the brand dealer service department with the VIN or use the OEM recall portal. |
| Import compliance | Review import documents and confirm Canadian compliance labels, daytime running lights, cluster units, and RIV status where applicable. |

### Dealer notes

Many recalls are free to complete at a franchise dealer, but timing matters. A vehicle waiting weeks for recall parts may tie up capital.

## Step 5: Research Retail Market Value

The target resale price should be based on comparable vehicles, not broad averages.

### Information to collect

- Similar vehicles currently listed
- Asking prices
- Mileage bands
- Trim differences
- Accident history differences
- Dealer vs private seller pricing
- Days on market where available
- Local supply and demand
- Seasonal demand

### How to access this information

| Information | How to access it |
| --- | --- |
| Retail listings | AutoTrader.ca, CarGurus Canada, Kijiji Autos/Kijiji, Facebook Marketplace, local dealer websites. |
| Asking price range | Filter comparable listings by year, trim, mileage, province, drivetrain, and accident status. |
| Days on market | CarGurus Canada, AutoTrader dealer tools where available, listing age, and saved-search monitoring. |
| Private seller pricing | Facebook Marketplace, Kijiji, and local classifieds. |
| Dealer retail pricing | AutoTrader.ca, franchise dealer sites, independent dealer sites, and CarGurus Canada. |
| Local demand | Listing count, message volume on similar listings, sold comparables if available, and auction activity. |

### Dealer notes

Comparable means genuinely comparable. A 2020 Honda Civic LX with accident history is not the same market as a 2020 Honda Civic Touring with clean history and lower mileage.

Useful comparison filters:

- Same year or one year newer/older
- Same trim or closest trim
- Similar mileage, usually within 15,000 to 25,000 km
- Same drivetrain
- Same province or nearby region
- Similar title status
- Similar accident history
- Similar color desirability

## Step 6: Research Wholesale and Trade-In Value

Wholesale value shows what dealers are likely paying, not what retail buyers are being asked to pay.

### Information to collect

- Auction sale prices
- Dealer trade-in value
- Wholesale bid range
- Reconditioning assumptions used by auction buyers
- Recent sale velocity

### How to access this information

| Information | How to access it |
| --- | --- |
| Wholesale value | Canadian Black Book, auction results, dealer appraisal tools, and internal dealership data. |
| Auction market | Manheim Canada, OPENLANE Canada, ADESA/TradeRev marketplace data where dealer access is available. |
| Trade-in value | Canadian Black Book, dealer appraisal software, and recent trade appraisals. |
| Dealer demand | Auction bidding activity, floor price movement, and number of similar vehicles crossing auction lanes. |

### Dealer notes

Retail asking price is not the buy price. The maximum buy price must be anchored to the realistic resale price minus all costs, risk, and target profit.

## Step 7: Estimate Reconditioning Costs

Reconditioning cost can make or break the deal.

### Information to collect

- Mechanical repairs
- Safety/certification requirements
- Tires
- Brakes
- Suspension
- Windshield
- Body and paint
- Detailing
- Interior repair
- Keys, remotes, manuals
- Diagnostic work
- Recall downtime

### How to access this information

| Information | How to access it |
| --- | --- |
| Mechanical condition | Physical inspection, test drive, OBD2 scan, pre-purchase inspection, mechanic estimate. |
| Safety requirements | Provincial safety inspection standards and licensed mechanic inspection. |
| Tire condition | Measure tread depth, check date codes, inspect wear pattern, price replacements from suppliers. |
| Brake condition | Visual inspection, road test, mechanic inspection, pad/rotor measurement. |
| Windshield/body damage | Physical inspection, glass quote, body shop quote, paint meter readings. |
| Detailing cost | Internal detailing rate or local detailer quote. |
| Key/remote replacement | Dealer parts department, locksmith quote, or OEM parts catalog. |
| Diagnostic codes | OBD2 scanner, professional diagnostic scan, mechanic report. |

### Dealer notes

Always separate "must fix before sale" from "nice to fix." Certification, safety, and buyer-confidence items come first.

## Step 8: Physically Inspect the Vehicle

Online research only tells you whether the car is worth inspecting.

### Inspection checklist

- Cold start
- Idle quality
- Engine noise
- Transmission shifts
- Clutch operation if manual
- Steering feel
- Suspension noise
- Brake feel and vibration
- Highway test
- City test
- Reverse gear
- AWD/4WD operation if applicable
- Air conditioning and heat
- Infotainment
- Cameras and sensors
- Power windows, locks, mirrors, seats
- Sunroof operation and leaks
- Rust underbody
- Frame rails
- Pinch welds
- Rocker panels
- Quarter panels
- Paint mismatch
- Panel gaps
- Overspray
- Water damage smell or staining
- Tire condition
- Fluid leaks
- OBD2 codes

### How to access this information

| Information | How to access it |
| --- | --- |
| Mechanical behavior | Test drive and mechanic inspection. |
| Hidden codes | OBD2 scan before and after test drive. |
| Rust and structural condition | Lift inspection, underbody photos, flashlight inspection, and body shop review when needed. |
| Accident repair quality | Paint meter, panel gap inspection, overspray check, weld/seam inspection, and body shop opinion. |
| Interior/electronics condition | Manual function test of every feature. |
| Water damage | Smell, carpets, spare tire well, seat tracks, electrical issues, corrosion under dash/seat areas. |

### Dealer notes

For accident-history vehicles, inspect structure carefully. A car can look clean in photos but still have poor repairs, corrosion, or alignment issues.

## Step 9: Calculate Maximum Buy Price

The maximum buy price should be calculated before negotiation.

### Formula

```text
Expected resale price
- safety/certification cost
- mechanical repairs
- tires/brakes
- body/glass/interior work
- detailing
- transport
- auction or buying fees
- advertising cost
- financing/floorplan cost
- warranty or after-sale risk reserve
- desired profit
= maximum buy price
```

### Example

```text
Expected resale price:        $18,500
Safety and repairs:           -$1,400
Selling costs and risk:         -$800
Target profit:                -$2,500
Maximum buy price:            $13,800
```

In this example, a dealer might start the offer around $12,500, try to land below $13,800, and walk away above that.

### How to access this information

| Information | How to access it |
| --- | --- |
| Expected resale price | Comparable retail listings, sold data where available, dealer experience, and local demand. |
| Repair costs | Mechanic quotes, parts suppliers, internal shop rates, body shop estimates. |
| Safety/certification cost | Licensed inspection facility or internal service department. |
| Transport cost | Tow company, auction transport quote, fuel/time estimate. |
| Auction/buying fees | Auction fee schedule or marketplace fee disclosure. |
| Advertising cost | Listing platform fees, paid boosts, photography, and internal marketing cost. |
| Financing/floorplan cost | Dealer floorplan provider, lender terms, or internal cost of capital. |
| Warranty risk reserve | Historical comeback rate, vehicle age/mileage, brand reliability, and warranty product cost. |
| Target profit | Dealer business model, vehicle price band, turnover speed, and risk level. |

### Dealer notes

Do not let the seller's asking price become the anchor. The maximum buy price is created by your resale math.

## Step 10: Classify the Vehicle

After research and inspection, put the vehicle into one of three buckets.

### Buy

Use this bucket when:

- History is clean or risk is clearly priced in
- No lien issue exists
- Demand is strong
- Reconditioning is manageable
- Margin is healthy
- Vehicle should sell within a reasonable timeline

### Buy Only Cheap

Use this bucket when:

- Accident claim exists
- Mileage is high
- Trim or color is less desirable
- Tires/brakes/repairs are needed
- Market is slower
- Margin exists only at a discounted buy price

### Pass

Use this bucket when:

- Branded title creates too much resale risk
- Flood, fire, or odometer concern exists
- Lien cannot be cleared safely
- Major mechanical risk exists
- Structural repair quality is poor
- Margin is too thin
- Vehicle will likely sit too long

### How to access this information

This classification is based on the combined evidence from:

- CARFAX Canada
- Provincial lien/title checks
- Transport Canada and OEM recall checks
- Retail listing research
- Wholesale/auction research
- Mechanic inspection
- Body inspection
- Test drive
- Dealer cost model

## Step 11: Negotiate the Purchase

Negotiation should follow the numbers.

### Information to use

- Comparable prices
- Vehicle history issues
- Repair estimates
- Safety/certification needs
- Tire/brake condition
- Market supply
- Days listed
- Seller urgency
- Your maximum buy price

### How to access this information

| Information | How to access it |
| --- | --- |
| Comparable prices | AutoTrader.ca, CarGurus Canada, Kijiji, Facebook Marketplace, dealer sites. |
| Vehicle defects | Inspection, mechanic quote, OBD2 scan, photos, and test drive. |
| Seller urgency | Listing age, seller communication, price drops, reason for selling. |
| Market supply | Count similar active listings in the region. |
| Maximum buy price | Your calculation from Step 9. |

### Dealer notes

If the maximum buy price is $13,800, do not pay $14,500 because the car "feels nice." That difference comes directly out of profit or risk reserve.

## Step 12: Plan the Resale Before Buying

A dealer should know the resale plan before purchasing.

### Information to decide

- Listing price
- Minimum acceptable selling price
- Whether to sell certified or as-is
- Repairs to complete before listing
- Target buyer
- Best listing platforms
- Photo strategy
- Description strategy
- Expected days to sell
- Backup wholesale exit price

### How to access this information

| Information | How to access it |
| --- | --- |
| Listing price | Retail comparable research and desired negotiation room. |
| Minimum sale price | Total cost basis plus minimum acceptable profit. |
| Certified vs as-is | Provincial rules, vehicle condition, target buyer, and margin. |
| Repair plan | Mechanic report, safety requirements, and buyer expectations. |
| Target buyer | Vehicle type, price band, financing needs, family/commercial/student use case. |
| Best platforms | AutoTrader.ca, Facebook Marketplace, Kijiji, dealer website, Google Business Profile, auction/wholesale channel. |
| Photo strategy | Competitive listings, vehicle condition, and buyer priorities. |
| Expected days to sell | Comparable listing age, demand, seasonality, and price competitiveness. |
| Wholesale exit | Auction values, dealer network, and wholesale buyer quotes. |

### Dealer notes

A vehicle with a strong retail story is easier to sell:

- Clean history
- Good service records
- Fresh safety
- Good tires and brakes
- Honest photos
- Clear description
- Competitive price

## Final Dealer Decision Checklist

Before buying, confirm:

- VIN matches all documents and the physical car.
- No unresolved lien or title problem exists.
- Accident/title history is acceptable for the intended resale strategy.
- Open recalls are understood and manageable.
- Comparable retail price is realistic.
- Wholesale value supports the buy price.
- Reconditioning costs are estimated conservatively.
- Inspection does not reveal hidden major risk.
- Maximum buy price is calculated.
- Target profit is worth the time and risk.
- Resale plan is clear before payment.

If any of these items are uncertain, either discount the offer heavily or pass.
